In the numerical experiment, we consider two types of graph learning tasks.
The first one is the subgraph alignment.
The subgraph alignment problem involves determining the isomorphism between a query graph and a subgraph of a larger target graph.
We can view the nodes in the large graph that are not in the query graph at outer layers and apply RGW to obtain the corresponding relationship.
We compare our methods with the methods for computing the original GW distance and some reverence of GW distance, including semi-relased GW, partial GW, and unbalanced GW.
Our method achieves the highest matching accuracy.
