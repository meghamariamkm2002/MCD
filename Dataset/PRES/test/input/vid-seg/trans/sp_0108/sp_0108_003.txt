Collaborative learning methods allow the training of machine learning models without requiring the use of large centralized silos of data.
Federated learning is a popular example of this paradigm, wherein many client processes communicate with a centralized server.
Rather than sending data directly to the server, clients send model updates based on their data.
This helps reduce costs and protect privacy.
However, it poses additional challenges.
For one, malicious clients may attempt to interfere with the training process by submitting intentionally poisonous or simply faulty updates.
On the other hand, clients must also still trust the server, as many previous works have shown that client data can be revealed by observing updates even when they're aggregated.
In our work, we propose methods which are robust to attacks from either side.

In particular, we propose the first collaborative learning protocol, guaranteed security against malicious clients and malicious servers.
