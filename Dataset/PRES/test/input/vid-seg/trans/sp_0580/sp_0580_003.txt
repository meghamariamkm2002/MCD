In this work, we look at the multi-objective minimum weight base problems, which falls within the realm of major automations.
Majority abstracts many combinatorial structures such as forests, mounds, machings, et cetera.
As the first step, we focus on the convex hull of the comparator front and divide a trade-off between approaches and guarantees and their upper size.
In particular, we use the structural properties of majorities to give results that are independent of objective values, which differs from previously known runtime upper bounds for general methods.
These are then used to analyze MOAD, a multiplicative evolutionary algorithms, in approximating the proto-fronts.
