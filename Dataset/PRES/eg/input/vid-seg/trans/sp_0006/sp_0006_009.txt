We propose an algorithm to recurrently estimate the interaction effects without knowing the polyhedral attention modules function.
In our algorithm, the function is regarded as a black box, and by changing elements of the input, the importance of the constant, main, and interaction effects
will be calculated via the output of the function.
In our experiment, we utilized the polyhedron technology to predict the brain age with the volume of the brain matter and rank the importance of the two-way interactions between the brain regions.
