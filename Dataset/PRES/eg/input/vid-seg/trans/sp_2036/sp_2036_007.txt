In the literature of weekly convex optimization, a common solution to such an issue is to use the gradient of the moral envelope as the measure of stability.
The merual envelope of the objective function is defined by an implicit smoothing function.
The small gradient of the merual envelope at w implies that w is near some point w hat that is nearly stationary of f. If the gradient of merual envelope at point w is upper bounded by epsilon, we call nearly epsilon stationary point of f.
