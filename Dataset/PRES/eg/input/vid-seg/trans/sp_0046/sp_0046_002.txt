In this paper, we focus on online convex optimization, which is a two-round game.
In each round, the learner summates a decision, and at the same time, the environments decide a convex loss function.
Later, the learner suffers loss of a decision and receives some feedback.
We choose regret as a performance measure, which is the excess cumulative loss against the best-fixed comparator.
Recent study is considered two levels of adaptivity.
At a higher level, the algorithm is required to be adaptive to unknown function curvature, and at a lower level, the algorithm is required to be adaptive to unknown environmental messiness.
