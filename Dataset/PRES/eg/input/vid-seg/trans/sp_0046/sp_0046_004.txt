The literature's environmental nestness is captured by some problem-dependent quantities.
In this paper, we focused on the gradient variation, which is accumulative changes between consecutive gradients.
Why do we study gradient variation?
On the one hand, it can exploit the nestness of environments and safeguard the worst case.
On the other hand, it draws profound connections with games and specificly extended serial models.
In games, the gradient of one player encodes other player's decisions.
Consider a simple two-layer zero sum game, such as a rock scissors paper game.
The X player receives a gradient of EYT, and the Y player receives a gradient of AXT.
The summation of the gradient variation bounds and makes cancellations if using algorithms with a negative stability term in analysis.
